\begin{abstract}
Vision Transformers (ViTs) have achieved remarkable performance across
various computer vision tasks, but their computational demands remain a
barrier to edge deployment. This paper systematically investigates the
compounding effect of structured pruning and post-training quantization
(PTQ) on lightweight ViT variants. We evaluate DeiT-Tiny, EfficientFormer-L1,
MobileViT-S, and EdgeViT-S under various pruning ratios (25--75\%) combined
with INT8 dynamic quantization. Our results show that combining 50\% head
pruning with PTQ reduces model size by up to 4$\times$ while maintaining
competitive accuracy, making these models viable for deployment on resource-
constrained devices such as the Raspberry Pi 4 and NVIDIA Jetson Nano.
\end{abstract}
